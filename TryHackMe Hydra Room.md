## Lab Entry Template

**Lab Title:**
Offensive Security Tooling - Hydra
**Platform/Provider:**  
TryHackMe
**Date Completed:**  
6/11/26
**Objectives:** 
Learn about and use Hydra, a fast network logon cracker, to bruteforce and obtain a website's credentials.
**Summary of Activities:**  
I completed the TryHackMe Hydra room by finding a valid login target, choosing the right attack method, and using Hydra with the proper service module and wordlist to test credentials. 
I got more comfortable adjusting the command syntax for the target service, watching the output for a successful login, and confirming the correct username and password. 
This lab helped reinforce how brute-force tools work in a controlled environment and reminded me how important it is to match the service, syntax, and wordlist to the situation when troubleshooting a login attempt.
**Skills/Tools Used:**
Hydra, Kali Linux
**Reflection:** 
This room gave me a much better understanding of how credential attacks work in a controlled lab environment and why being precise matters when using Hydra. 
It reinforced that success comes from choosing the right service, formatting the command correctly, and paying close attention to the output instead of guessing. 
More than anything, it reminded me that this kind of testing is about discipline and process, not rushing — start with the right target, verify your assumptions, and troubleshoot carefully. 
That’s the mindset I want to keep sharpening as I continue building my cybersecurity skills.
